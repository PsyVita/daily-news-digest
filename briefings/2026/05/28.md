## Fun fact

Roman concrete structures like the Pantheon have survived two millennia while modern concrete crumbles in decades — and one reason is that Roman concrete repairs itself. The Romans mixed quicklime directly into their mortar, leaving small calcium-rich clasts scattered through the material. When a crack forms and rainwater seeps in, it dissolves these lime clasts, and the resulting calcium-saturated solution recrystallizes as calcium carbonate, sealing the crack like a scab. For centuries engineers dismissed the white lumps as sloppy mixing; in 2023, MIT researchers showed they were a feature, not a flaw. Modern "self-healing" concrete startups are now copying a recipe that was state of the art before the year 100. [Source](https://example.com/roman-concrete)
