## Fun fact

The Nopoli rock-climbing goby, a finger-length fish native to Hawaii, scales 100-meter waterfalls — the human equivalent of free-climbing a cliff three times the height of Everest. It does this with two suction cups: one formed from fused pelvic fins, and a second, remarkably, made from its mouth. The fish inches upward by alternating suckers, a technique it also uses for scraping algae off rocks — biologists still debate whether climbing evolved from feeding or the other way around. Why bother? Waterfalls are a security feature: the pools above them are free of predators, so the hardest climb wins the safest nursery. [Source](https://example.com/climbing-goby)
