## Fun fact
With SpaceX completing the largest IPO in history today, it's worth remembering the very first one: in 1602, the Dutch East India Company (VOC) invited the public to buy shares in its spice-trading voyages, becoming the world's first company to issue stock to ordinary citizens. Over a thousand investors subscribed — including a handful of servants and maids — and a secondary market for trading the shares sprang up almost immediately, effectively creating the Amsterdam Stock Exchange. The VOC went on to become arguably the most valuable enterprise ever; adjusted estimates of its peak worth run to several trillion dollars in today's money, dwarfing even modern tech giants. Shareholders sometimes received dividends paid not in cash but in cloves, mace, and pepper. [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Dutch-East-India-Company)
