## Fun fact

The QWERTY keyboard layout wasn't designed to slow typists down — that's a myth. Christopher Latham Sholes arranged keys in the 1870s to keep frequently-paired type bars apart so they wouldn't jam when struck in quick succession; it was about mechanics, not friction. The arrangement also helped salesmen: all the letters of "TYPE WRITER" sit on the top row, perfect for fast demos. By the time jam-proof designs and supposedly faster layouts like Dvorak arrived, millions of typists had trained on QWERTY, and the cost of retraining outweighed any speed gain. Economists call it path dependence — a 150-year-old fix for a mechanical problem that no longer exists, preserved forever by muscle memory. [Source](https://example.com/qwerty)
